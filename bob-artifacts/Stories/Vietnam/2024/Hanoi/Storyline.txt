Hanoi, Vietnam - Getting Lost in the Heart of the City

Hanoi was never about checking off a list of tourist spots for me. It was about slowing down, walking without a plan, and letting the city reveal itself one street at a time.

I spent my days wandering around the Old Quarter, where every corner felt alive — from the endless sound of motorbikes passing by, small local shops hidden in narrow alleys, to street vendors preparing food right in front of you. The city had this beautiful chaos that somehow felt comforting.

One of my favorite parts of Hanoi was discovering its food culture. I went on a little food adventure — trying different egg coffees, searching for the perfect Bahn Mi, and walking around to find that unforgettable bowl of Phở that captures the true taste of Vietnam.

There was something special about sitting in a small café, holding a cup of Vietnamese coffee, watching locals go about their day. Simple moments, but the kind of memories that make traveling worth it.

Hanoi reminded me that sometimes the best way to experience a place is not through an itinerary, but by getting lost — following random streets, trying local flavors, and collecting stories along the way.

A few days in Hanoi, but a lifetime of memories.✨
